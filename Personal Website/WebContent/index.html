ABOUT ME

Welcome to my website.

My name is, obviously, Muhammad Moneib, and I am a software engineer by profession as well as an amateur artist, writer, and traveller -- or that's what I'd rather believe.

I was born in Cairo, Egypt back in 1984, which happens to be the title of my favorite book so far. There's so much to learn about the current world from Orwell's novel, especially if you are a defensive pessimist like myself. The idea of defensive pessimism is to expect the worst while you hope for the best, which will protect you from disappointment and unwelcomed surprises, hence the defensive part.

This I have learned during my thirty-something years on this planet the hard way, through trial and error, which is the same mechanism I have employed to get the hang of all my self-taught leisure skills, including photgraphy, writing, and drawing, among others.

I started experimenting with photography back in 2002 when I first owned a mobile phone with an embedded camera. The idea of virtual storage and how cheap and accessible it made photography fascinated me, and despite the humble quality of the images at that time, they were more than enough for me as a creative outlet to explore my artistic side..

Then, in 2007, I began ptactising drawing on regular basis, which turned out to be my true passion. First, it was the challenge to get the likeness of people on paper that drew me to this hobby, but soon enough I found purer enjoyment in expressing myself and docoumenting my dreams visually.

Through constant practising, I was able to improve my drawing skills significantly, not only with the quality of the lines, but with also the fluidity of the ideas. For me, practice doesn't make perfect, but it, most certainly, makes better.

In the world of arts, I have found something much better than immitating reality; I have found it a very effective outlet for my most nagging thoughts and a soother of the most notorious of my inner demons, demands which even writing, with all its therapeutical effects, couldn't satisfy. Yet through writing I am able to document and communicate the details of my abstract ideas for my own consumption before anyone else's.

My relationship with computers started in 1994, just one year before I have written my first computer program. Five years later, I was selling software over the internet for Palm devices under the name of an imaginary company, so it was natural for me to pursue my higher studies in computer and software engineering.

Nevertheless, I perceive technoligy, per se, as a dillemma each day: something which is supposed to make lives simpler is clearly not. We are trading, rather idiotically, the wealth of nature with the false promises of the virtual worlds, with greed as its precursor. Yet it doesn't have to be that way. This flase dichtomy can be put to rest as humans drift away from competition towards cooperation, maintain a steady population, and discover new ways to immerse reality into technology.

No matter where one lives, we are facing the same challenges, albeit in different incarnations. My solo travels around the world have taught me that despite our differences, we are all the same, that we should get together to tackle our common dangers, and that we should be conservationists rather than conservatives in a way that can adjourn the much needed liberal mindset with the ornaments of the common good.

Hence, the challenges for unification and cooperation are immense, whether at the global or personal levels. It is my desire to bring this reality into technology and into art as much as I can. Be it through drawing, photography, writing, coding...etc., what is important is to work in a certain direction with whatever tools one has got. One of these tools is this website, which I am building for myself in the first place to enhance my front-end development skills in addition to collecting and showcasing my different works in a single place. I hope it would be beneficial to some others as well.

If you have made it that far through reading every word, then thanks for your time. I believe my freewriting exercise for today is over.

Muhammad Moneib
