ABOUT ME

Welcome to my website.

My name is, obviously, Muhammad Moneib, and I am a software engineer by profession as well as an amateur artist, writer, and traveller -- or that's what I'd rather believe.

I was born in Cairo, Egypt back in 1984, which happens to be the title of my favorite book so far. There's so much to learn about the current world from Orwell's novel, especially if you are a defensive pessimist like myself. The idea of defensive pessimism is to expect the worst while you hope for the best, which will protect you from disappointment and unwelcomed surprises, hence the defensive part.

This I have learned during my thirty-something years on this planet the hard way, through trial and error, which is the same mechanism I have employed to get the hang of all my self-taught skill, including photgraphy, writing, and drawing, among others.

For instance, I have always been fascinated by people who seemed to be so talented in producing the likeness of people on paper, and despite several trials in my late teens, I failed to make anything that I could deem successful, but it got me hooked on the task nevertheless. Photography being easier, I started taking photos with my mobile camera back in 2002, but it wasn't until 2007 when I focused on drawing regularly, something which I have been doing ever since.

For an escavator of my old skertches from that date, he would find drawings that could be done by a child, let alone a 23 years old, that if he compares them to my current drawings, he would conclude decisively that practice doesn't make perfect, but it, most certainly, makes better.

In the world of arts, I have found something much better than immitating reality in the form of people's likeness; I have found a very effective outlet for my most nagging thoughts and a soother of the most notorious of my inner demons, demands which even writing, with all its therapeutical effects, couldn't satisfy. Yet through writing I am able to document and communicate the details of my abstract ideas for my own consumption before anyone else's.

I have had a handful of articles and essays published online, and a poem publsihed in an online literary magazine -- yes, sometimes I write poems and I am not ashamed of it -- but the dearest writings to me are those touching the the personal side of things, which remind myeself of a moment I have lived, or a book I have read, or an idea that just sparked inside my brain, not to mention a code that does something useful.

Sitting in front of a computer for a living can be a very boring thing, unless you put it for a good use. Technoligy, per se, is becoming more of a dillemma each day, something which is supposed to make lives simpler is clearly not. We seem to have everything in the palm of our hand, but in reality we have nothing but some silicon chips and nagging sounds. We are trading, rather idiotically, the wealth of nature with the false promises of the virtual worlds, with greed as the precursor for this. Ironically enough, we are literally constructing our own destruction, unless something is done quickly to change the status-quo.

That's how I feel connected with earth. Being a tech guy who loves nature and recognizes the dangers of technology on it, I too feel the irony. Yet it doesn't have to be that way. This flase dichtomy can be put to rest as humans drift away from competition towards cooperation, maintain a steady population, and discover new ways to immerse reality into technology. Humans would do much better if they too embrace the defensive pessimist's attitude.

No matter where I go, I find the same patterns, albeit in different incarnations. I have travelled solely to much of Europe and some of the middle-east, and, despite the different cultures and quality of lives, I have spotted the same expressions, the same worries, and even the same prejudices. We are not that different if we look deep enough, and that should be enough to get us together to tackle our common dangers. Yet inspite of the geographical closeness between many of these places -- and even within the same societies -- there remain tensions which are clear as the contrast becomes stark between the developed and the developing world, between the green and arid lands, and between the liberal and conservative mindsets.

Hence, the challenges for unification and cooperation are immense, whether at the global or personal levels. It is my desire to bring this reality into technology and into art as much as I can. Be it through drawing, photography, writing, coding...etc., what is important is to work in a certain direction with w_1hatever tools one has got. One of these tools is this website, which I am building for myself in the first place to enhance my front-end development skills in addition to collecting and showcasing my different works in a single place. I hope it would be beneficial to some others as well.

If you have made it that far through reading every word, then thanks for your time. I believe my freewriting exercise for today is over.

Muhammad Moneib
